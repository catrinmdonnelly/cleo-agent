# Example state.md

*This is what a strong `state.md` looks like for a fictional ecommerce brand. Yours doesn't need to be polished prose; bullet points are fine. The point is specificity. Real numbers, named channels, actual situations.*

---

## This month at a glance

- Revenue: £4,200 across 38 orders. Average order £110.
- 3 customers came back for a second order, biggest repeat number we've ever had in a month.
- Instagram traffic flat, sessions down 12% month on month.
- Newsletter open rate 41%, click rate 8%. The "behind the scenes" angle outperforms product launches by 3x.
- Working on launching the new mug range in 3 weeks. Stuck on which photographer to use, three quotes in front of me.

## Last month for comparison

- £3,800 revenue, 32 orders. Same average order value.
- Newsletter open rate was 38%. Slightly up this month.
- Instagram was actually up 5% last month, before the dip.

## What we're testing right now

- A "field notes" newsletter that's just a paragraph from me about what I made that week, no products, no CTA. Open rate is the highest of any newsletter we've sent.
- Started a TikTok account 3 weeks ago. 4 posts, 800 total views, no sales attributed yet.

## What we know works

- Email above all. Every successful launch starts with a newsletter.
- The "behind the scenes / how it's made" angle on any channel
- Customers who buy a second time spend on average 40% more than first-time customers
- Photo posts on Instagram still outperform Reels for our audience (small craft buyers, mid-30s+)

## What we know doesn't work

- Tried Pinterest in February. 14k impressions, zero clicks. Stopped.
- Tried paid Instagram ads in March, £200 spent for 1 attributed sale. Stopped.
- Generic "shop now" CTAs in any channel. Audience finds them off-putting.
- Posting more than 3 times a week on Instagram. Engagement crashes.

## Money

- Cash in bank: £18,000
- Monthly burn: £4,500 (mostly materials, rent on workshop, my own salary)
- Runway at flat revenue: 4 months

## Anything else

- Mother's Day is in 4 weeks. No campaign planned yet, this is the biggest gift-giving moment of the year for us. Last year we did £8k that week.
- Customer feedback theme this month: people want gift boxes. Three people emailed asking. We don't currently sell gift boxes.
- Considering whether to spend £600 on commissioning a brand video, or £600 on paid Instagram ads. Leaning toward video because organic Instagram is dying for us.
- I'm tired. Solo founder, full-time on this since January. Need to think about whether I'm spreading myself too thin.
