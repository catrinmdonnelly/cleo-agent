# Cleo's config

Cleo reads three files in this folder every Monday. The agent's quality depends almost entirely on what's in them.

| File | What it's for | When to update |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| `state.md` | **The most important file.** What's happening in your business right now. Revenue, channels, what's working, what isn't, what's launching, what's stuck. | Whenever something material changes. At minimum, every couple of weeks. |
| `north-star.md` | The big picture. Where the business is going. Who it's for. The things that don't change. | Once at setup. Revisit every 6 months. |
| `memory.md` | Lessons across runs. "Tried X in March, it didn't work because Y." | Optional. Can start empty. Add entries over time. |
| `system-prompt.md` | Cleo's persona. How she writes, what she pays attention to. | Optional. Leave blank to use the default. |

## How to fill them in

Start with `state.md`. See [state.example.md](state.example.md) in this folder for a worked example showing what good looks like.

The more specific you are, the sharper Cleo's brief. "Things are going okay" is weak input. "£4,200 revenue across 38 orders last month, newsletter open rate 41%, the behind-the-scenes angle outperforms product launches 3x" is strong input.

Cleo doesn't need polished prose. Bullet points and fragments are fine. She'll work with what's there.

## Don't have data yet?

If you're a new business and don't have months of numbers to work with, fill in `state.md` with what you do have:
- What you've been doing this week, in plain English
- Anything you've noticed about who's interested
- What's stuck, what's slow, what's anxious-making
- What you'd like to be true in 3 months

Cleo's briefs will be lighter to start with, but she'll still help you stay focused. She gets sharper as you give her more to react to.
